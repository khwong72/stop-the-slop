# Banned Phrases & Patterns

Check every sentence against these patterns. Any match = rewrite.

---

## Sentence Structures (NEVER USE)

| Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---------|--------------|
| "X isn't Y, it's Z" | Overused contrast formula |
| "This isn't just X, it's Y" | False depth |
| "They're not just X, they're Y" | Hollow reframe |
| "No X. No Y. Just Z." | Fragment pairs for fake emphasis |
| "While everyone else was doing X, [company] was doing Y" | Strawman comparison |
| "You're either X or Y" | False dichotomy |
| "That's not X — that's Y" | Em-dash dramatic reframe |

---

## Transitions (NEVER USE)

- "Here's the kicker"
- "Here's why this matters"
- "But here's the thing"
- "Here's the thing"
- "The pattern is clear"
- "The bottom line"
- "At the end of the day"
- "This is where it gets interesting"
- "Let me explain"

---

## Dramatic Language (NEVER USE)

- Transform / Transformation / Revolutionary / Game-changer
- Crushing it / Killing it / Dominating
- The secret to... / The hidden truth about...
- Everything changed when...
- The companies that win...
- The future belongs to...

---

## Coined Terms (NEVER USE)

- "[X] engine" (trust engine, growth engine)
- "[X] at scale" (relationships at scale)
- "People don't buy from [X], they buy from [Y]"
- Abstract nouns as competitive advantages ("authenticity", "trust")

---

## Openings (NEVER USE)

- "Most people think..."
- "Everyone talks about X, but nobody mentions Y"
- "I used to believe X. Then I learned Y."
- "Here's what nobody tells you about..."

---

## Closings (NEVER USE)

- "X won't replace Y, but it will change Z"
- "The future is here, and it's..."
- "Are you ready for X?"
- Any question that sounds rhetorical

### Profound Closers (NEVER USE)

- "The companies winning right now aren't just [X]. They're [Y]."
- "In a world where [X], [Y] is the new [Z]."
- "[X] can't buy that. [Y] can't replicate it."
- Any closing that sounds like a keynote finale

**Instead:** End with what to do, or just stop.

---

## Formatting (NEVER USE)

- Emoji category headers
- Em dashes for dramatic pauses (use periods)
- Template patterns (same structure repeated)

---

## The Test

Read it aloud. If it sounds like:
- A LinkedIn guru → Rewrite
- Someone performing expertise → Rewrite

If it sounds like:
- A founder explaining over coffee → You're close
