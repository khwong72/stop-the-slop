# The 10 Patterns

AI tells that standard avoid lists miss. Check each one.

---

## 1. False Agency

AI avoids naming actors by giving objects human verbs.

| Slop | Clean |
|------|-------|
| "The complaint becomes a fix" | "The team fixed it that week" |
| "The decision emerges" | "She decided" |
| "The culture shifts" | "People changed how they work" |
| "The data tells us" | "We saw in the data" |
| "The market rewards" | "Buyers pay more for" |

**Check:** Is an inanimate thing doing a human verb? Name the human.

---

## 2. Narrator Voice

Floating above the scene instead of putting the reader in it.

| Slop | Clean |
|------|-------|
| "Nobody designed this" | "You don't sit down and plan this" |
| "This happens because..." | "You see this when..." |
| "People tend to..." | "You probably..." |

**Check:** Are you lecturing from distance? Put the reader in the room.

---

## 3. Wh- Openers

Sentences starting with What/When/Where/Which/Who/Why/How become a crutch.

| Slop | Clean |
|------|-------|
| "What makes this hard is..." | "The hard part is..." |
| "Why this matters is..." | State why directly |
| "How this works is..." | Just explain how it works |
| "When you think about it..." | Cut entirely |

**Check:** Does a sentence start with a Wh- word? Restructure.

---

## 4. Vague Declaratives

Announcing importance without naming the specific thing.

| Slop | Problem |
|------|---------|
| "The reasons are structural" | What structure? Name it. |
| "The implications are significant" | What implications? List them. |
| "The stakes are high" | What stakes? Be specific. |
| "This is the deepest problem" | What problem? Say it. |

**Check:** If you can't name the specific thing, cut the sentence.

---

## 5. Negative Runway

Listing what something is NOT before saying what it IS.

| Slop | Clean |
|------|-------|
| "Not a consultant. Not a vendor. A partner." | "A partner." |
| "It wasn't luck. It wasn't timing. It was preparation." | "It was preparation." |
| "Not because X. Because Y." | "Because Y." |

**Check:** Are you building up through negation? Skip the runway.

---

## 6. Three-Item Lists

Three items feel AI-generated. Two feels human.

| Slop | Clean |
|------|-------|
| "Speed. Quality. Cost." | "Speed and quality." |
| "Plan, execute, iterate." | "Execute and iterate." |
| "Think, build, ship." | "Build and ship." |

**Check:** Do you have three items in sequence? Use two or one.

---

## 7. Question Endings

Ending with a question feels like a LinkedIn CTA.

| Slop | Clean |
|------|-------|
| "What would you do differently?" | End on the insight |
| "Are you doing this?" | State what to do |
| "Does this resonate?" | Just stop |

**Check:** Does your post end with a question? End on truth or action instead.

---

## 8. Over-Explanation

Explaining things the reader already understands.

| Slop | Why It Fails |
|------|--------------|
| "OODA stands for Observe, Orient, Decide, Act" | They'll look it up |
| Defining common terms in parentheses | Trust the reader |
| Restating the same point in different words | One statement is enough |

**Check:** Would the reader get this without the explanation? Cut it.

---

## 9. Adverb Infestation

All -ly words are suspect. Most should die.

**Kill these:**
- really, just, literally, genuinely, honestly, simply, actually
- deeply, truly, fundamentally, inherently, inevitably
- interestingly, importantly, crucially, essentially

**Also kill:**
- "At its core"
- "When it comes to"
- "The reality is"
- "It's worth noting"

**Check:** Can you remove the adverb without changing meaning? Remove it.

---

## 10. Passive Hiding

Passive voice hides who did the thing.

| Slop | Clean |
|------|-------|
| "Mistakes were made" | "We made mistakes" |
| "The decision was reached" | "The team decided" |
| "It was believed that" | "They believed" |

**Check:** Every sentence needs a human subject doing something.
